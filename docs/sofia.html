Challenge

Our designers team delved into understanding our clients' needs and made an intriguing observation: each client had unique preferences for managing their data. However, amidst this diversity, a common thread ran through their dashboard ideas. Recognizing this pattern, we decided to create a universal template that could seamlessly accommodate the individual needs of each client. This initiative not only streamlined our company's processes but also ensured efficiency across all projects.

Exploring the Problem

As we started our research, our team identified two primary challenges that required resolution:

Making the template suitable for different clients:
We needed to create a structure that could work well for a variety of businesses.

Keeping it customizable:
At the same time, we had to ensure that clients could tweak the template to suit their individual needs.

Interview

In our quest to refine our data management solution, we reviewed the input from our loyal customers. Using their feedback as a guide, we conducted interviews with new clients, keen to grasp their specific requirements.

Here are the key statements we noticed:
Accessible Information:
Customers prioritize a dashboard that's easy to navigate and visually appealing, catering to users of all levels while reflecting their brand identity.
Customizable Features:
They seek a template with customizable fields and automated workflows to streamline data management tasks, adapting to their evolving business needs.
Compliance and Security:
Dashboards must adhere to GDPR and HIPAA regulations, ensuring stringent data security while embodying a modern, user-friendly design.

Information Architecture

In preparation for our design endeavor, we embarked on a thorough analysis of our past projects and engaged in insightful interviews with clients. Having meticulously crafted a variant of the information architecture, we had obtained invaluable insights essential for initiating the design process effectively.

Foundations: system and tokens

As we laid the foundations for our project, our first step was to create a comprehensive design system with a token-based approach to ensure consistency and scalability. It featured responsive grids, a curated color palette with primary, secondary, and semantic colors, and reusable UI components and modular elements. Design tokens managed colors, typography, spacing, and border radii, providing a single source of truth for styling. This streamlined development, ensured visual consistency, and enabled quick customization for various user needs.

Widgets

With our design system established, we began compiling a list of essential widgets. We carefully considered user needs and industry standards to ensure that each widget would enhance functionality and user experience. Our goal was to create a straightforward yet versatile collection that would streamline data management tasks and provide valuable insights for users across different business scenarios.
